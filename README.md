## 🔍 Market Persona Mapping
I divided the market into three distinct personas based on quantitative decision drivers rather than just industry size:

* **Segment A: Price-Driven Small Commercial (35%)**
    * *Drivers:* Initial Cost + Immediate Availability.
    * *Strategy:* Entry-level product launch to disrupt L&T's dominance.
* **Segment B: Reliability-Driven Industrial (45%)**
    * *Drivers:* Uptime + Warranty + Technical Support.
    * *Strategy:* "Warranty Visibility" campaigns to leverage Schneider's inherent strengths.
* **Segment C: Project-Driven Infrastructure (20%)**
    * *Drivers:* Certifications + Supply Chain Track Record.
    * *Strategy:* Reference-based selling and localized advantage positioning.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology: The Win-Loss Deep Dive
I conducted a rigorous analysis of **100 closed deals** (52 Won, 48 Lost) to uncover internal and external friction points:

### ✅ Schneider Strengths
* High product availability in the Small Commercial segment.
* Superior warranty perception in Industrial applications.

### ❌ Critical Weaknesses
* **Price Perception:** Over-priced in Segment B despite comparable value.
* **Awareness Gap:** Low market penetration/awareness in high-stakes Infrastructure projects (Segment C).

---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations
1.  **"Best Value" Positioning:** Re-branded Schneider as the "Value-Premium" leader—offering local advantages that global premium brands lack.
2.  **Product Roadmap:** Proposed an entry-level product tier specifically to capture Segment A market share currently held by L&T.
3.  **Sales Playbooks:** Created segment-specific "Win Strategies" including price-matching thresholds and reference-selling templates.
